## Idea
 - Grand Strategy Chess
 - Uses traditional chess move set and rules
 - The board has resources which can be harvested for gold
 - Gold can be spent on units, tech, or buildings
 - Tech tree enhances units, buildings, resource gathering, etc
 - Buildings can be built on any non-resource tile, but if captured are destroyed.
 - All units can build or use their special action. Only the unit that is moved may do these things though
 - Get Bonus tech for taking units.
 - Will likely not use the normal chess board size, may even have custom shapes which means that units may need some changes to their movement (i.e pawns)
 
 ## 1. MOVEMENT & COMBAT

- All units retain **traditional chess movement** unless modified by tech.
    
- **Capture rules remain purely chess**: move into a square occupied by an enemy piece.
    
- Capturing an enemy piece grants **+1 Tech**.
    

---

# **2. THE BOARD**

Instead of the classic 8×8:

- The board may be **larger**, **irregular**, or **thematically shaped** (continent map, ring, spokes, etc.)
    
- Tiles are one of:
    
    - **Resource tile** (Mine, Farm, Forest, Town, etc.)
        
    - **Buildable tile** (empty land)
        
    - **Base tile** (starting zone)
        
    - **Special tile** (teleport, choke point, high ground, etc.) (Optional)
        

### Movement on custom boards:

- **Knights** ignore shape irregularities — jump as usual.
    
- **Pawns**:
    
    - “Forward” is any straight direction because of potential irregular board shapes.
        
    - Promotion rules adapt to your map (promotion zone = enemy half or enemy HQ).
        
    - Pawns still capture diagonally.
        
- **Sliding pieces** (bishop/rook/queen):
    
    - Move until blocked, using the board’s geometry.
        

---

# **3. RESOURCES & GOLD**

Each **resource tile** produces **1 gold** when harvested.  
Some tiles give more:

| Tile Type  | Gold/Harvest | Notes                                                     |
| ---------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Farm**   | +1           | Basic economy                                             |
| **Mine**   | +2           | Slower to reach, high value, typically in middle of board |
| **Town**   | +1 +1 Tech   | Good mid-game boost                                       |
| **Forest** | +1           | Also grants +1 defence if tech unlocked                   |

Gold is used for:

- **Recruiting units**
    
- **Constructing buildings**
    
- **Buying tech upgrades**
    

---

# **4. UNITS**

You start with classic chess pieces.  
Additional units (optional) can be purchased.

### **Default Piece Roles**

All units may:

- **Build** (if moved that turn)
    
- Use their **special action** (if moved that turn)
    
- **Harvest** (if moved onto a resource tile)
    
### **Pawn**

- Best harvester: **+1 bonus gold** on Farms.
    
- Promotion still exists.
    

### **Knight**

- Special action: **Charge** (+1 capture power).
    
- Best Offensive unit
    

### **Bishop**

- Aura: adjacent friendly pieces get **+1 defence**.
    
- Defensive unit
    

### **Rook**

- Special action: **Fortify (Lock)**
    
    - Cannot move for 1 turn
        
    - Cannot be captured during that same turn
        
- Used to anchor fronts.    

### **Queen**

- Can use ANY special action by late game. Early Game no abilities.
    
- Also the best unit to lead assaults or boost production.
    

### **King**

- Pure victory unit (you may or may not keep checkmate rules).
    
- Can harvest any tile for **all resources** (Gold + Tech + whatever).
	
- Does not get any defensive bonuses.
    




---

# **5. BUILDINGS**

Built on **non-resource** tiles. If the tile is captured, the building is **destroyed**.

### Basic Buildings

- **Outpost** (cheap): +1 defence to tile
    
- **Mine** (buildable version): produce 1 gold every turn
    
- **Barracks**: Alternative Unit Spawn Location
    
- **Library/School**: +1 Tech per turn
    

### Building Rules

- Only the unit that **moved** may build.
    
- Building costs gold.
    
- Advanced buildings require tech levels.
    

---

# **6. TECH TREE**

Tech is simple:  
You have **Tech Level**, and each unlock increases your capabilities.

### Sample Tech Tree

**Tech I**

- +1 gold from Farms
    
- Pawns can build Outposts at half cost
    
- Unlocks Barracks
    

**Tech II**

- Rook Lock lasts up to 2 turns
    
- Harvest Mines for +3 instead of +2
    
- +1 Defence to Outposts
    

**Tech III**

- Pawns +1 Movement (including diagonally)
    
- Build Mines
    
- Harvest Towns for +2 Gold +1 Tech
    

**Tech IV (Late-game)**

- Queens gain second move
    
- Recruit advanced units (Cannons, Elephants, etc.)
    
- Build Citadels (super-defensive)
    

You also gain **+1 Tech anytime you capture a piece**.

This creates cool opening strategies:

- Aggressive → tech rush through captures
    
- Economic → harvest and build
    
- Hybrid → build libraries and pressure opponents
    

---

# **7. TURN STRUCTURE**

Each turn:

### **1. Move a Unit**

Perform a **legal chess move**.

### **2. Choose ONE LEGAL Action for That Unit**

- **Harvest resource** 
    
- **Build**
    
- **Use special action**

## **3. Upgrade Tech**

- **Choose a tech to unlock**

This keeps turns fast and reduces clutter.

---

# **8. WIN CONDITIONS**

Choose one or mix:

### **1. Checkmate (Classic)**

Checkmate ends the game.

### **2. Capital Capture**

Capture the enemy Capital by sieging it down. (3 turns?)

### **3. Tech Victory**

Unlock Nuclear Weapons

### **4. Domination (Alternate option)**

Control majority of resource tiles/territories.

This lets the game be played seriously, competitively, or casually depending on map size.
